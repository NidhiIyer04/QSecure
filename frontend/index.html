// Why It Matters
THE QUANTUM CLOCK IS TICKING
Shor's algorithm runs on a quantum computer and factors large numbers exponentially faster than any classical machine. Every RSA key, every elliptic curve — done in seconds.
Critical Threat
Shor's Algorithm Breaks RSA & ECDSA
A large enough quantum computer runs Shor's algorithm to factor the product of two huge primes in polynomial time — the same problem that makes RSA secure. RSA-2048 falls in hours. ECDSA falls too, via the elliptic curve discrete log problem. Both are gone the moment a cryptographically-relevant quantum computer exists.
01
Active Right Now
"Harvest Now, Decrypt Later"
Nation-state actors are already collecting your encrypted traffic today. When quantum computers mature, they'll decrypt everything retroactively.
02
Solution Exists
PQC Standards Are Ready
NIST finalized three post-quantum standards in Aug 2024: ML-KEM, ML-DSA, and SLH-DSA. Drop-in replacements. The only blocker is migrating your code.
03
Affects Hashing
Grover's Algorithm Halves Symmetric Keys
Even symmetric crypto is affected. Grover's algorithm turns AES-128 into effective AES-64. AES-256 stays safe — but SHA-1 and MD5 are finished.
04
Start Today
The 10-Year Migration Window
Large organisations need 10–15 years to migrate crypto infrastructure. IBM, Google, and NIST agree: the migration must start now or you won't make it in time.
