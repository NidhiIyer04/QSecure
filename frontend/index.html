// Why It Matters
THE QUANTUM CLOCK IS TICKING
Shor's algorithm runs on a quantum computer and factors large numbers exponentially faster than any classical machine. Every RSA key, every elliptic curve — broken in hours.
Critical Threat
Shor's Algorithm Breaks RSA & ECDSA
A sufficiently large quantum computer runs Shor's algorithm to factor the product of two primes in polynomial time — the hardness assumption underlying RSA. The same algorithm also solves the elliptic curve discrete logarithm problem, making ECDSA and ECDH equally broken. RSA-2048 becomes trivially solvable in hours.
Shor (1994) — Algorithms for Quantum Computation · NIST IR 8105

"Harvest Now, Decrypt Later"
Nation-state actors are already collecting encrypted traffic today to decrypt once quantum computers mature. Your data from today, exposed tomorrow.
CISA Quantum Advisory · NSA CNSA 2.0

Solution Exists
PQC Standards Are Ready
NIST finalized three post-quantum standards August 2024: ML-KEM (FIPS 203), ML-DSA (FIPS 204), SLH-DSA (FIPS 205). Drop-in replacements exist today.
FIPS 203 · FIPS 204 · FIPS 205

Grover's Algorithm Halves Symmetric Keys
Grover's algorithm provides a quadratic speedup on brute-force search — AES-128 becomes effectively AES-64. AES-256 remains safe. SHA-1 and MD5 are fully broken.
Grover (1996) · NIST SP 800-131A
